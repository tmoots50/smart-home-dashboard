# UI Audit — 2026-04-29

## Snapshot
Three full-page screenshots of `localhost:5173`, captured in landscape (~1400px wide). Production target is **portrait 1080×1920**; the aspect-ratio mismatch shapes most findings below — anything contingent on type size, gutter width, or density is an estimate until reflow.

## Top 5 fixes (across all domains, ranked by leverage)

1. **Reflow for portrait.** The dashboard is being designed and judged in landscape, but the cocopar runs portrait 1080×1920. Until it's rebuilt against the production viewport, every other measurement is provisional. This is the precondition for the rest of the audit being actionable rather than directional.

2. **Pick one morning-fold view.** The page currently scrolls roughly three full vertical screens. A wall display has no scroll affordance, so two-thirds of what's been built is invisible to anyone glancing past it. Decide what fits one portrait screen for the morning, and demote the rest to a second view (evening, auto-rotated by time of day, or tap-reveal).

3. **Stop letting the clock dominate.** `11:25 PM` is the largest element on screen, but a bathroom has other clocks. The headline slot should go to the highest-information widget — next event, Mabel status, or the daily note. Shrink the clock 30–40% and promote a content widget into the visual lead.

4. **Bump touch targets.** Checkboxes in Today and Chores read ~24px square. Apple HIG floor is 44; kiosk-research consensus for wall displays is 60+. From a wet-handed bathroom tap, 24px is miss-prone. Bump to ≥48 with the tap-area extending across the whole row, not just the checkbox glyph.

5. **Fix two info-design tells that silently erode trust.** "Halfway through." is opaque without a referent — anchor it ("Day 24 of paternity leave," "Week 4 with Mabel," whatever's true) or drop it. Today's Note is undated, so a stale 3-day-old note looks identical to today's; add a small `· this morning` / `· yesterday` timestamp so the daily heartbeat is visible.

## Domain notes

### 1. Layout & hierarchy
Twelve widgets at near-uniform visual weight; no single focal point wins the squint test. The greeting block reserves a full-width banner for one sentence — a lot of vertical real estate at low information density. Top-left is occupied by warm but low-information greeting copy; per Few, that slot earns the highest-priority info, not vibe alone.

### 2. Typography — STRENGTH
Portfolio-grade. Editorial display serif (Tiempos / Domaine family) paired with a humanist sans, italics deployed purposefully, hierarchy carried by size+weight not just color. Above the v1 bar by a wide margin — preserve through any rebuild. Two minor notes: italic serif now appears in four places (greeting sub, note, verse, photo caption) and is diluting the emphasis it should carry; calendar event times need tabular numerals so the time column aligns cleanly.

### 3. Color & contrast — STRENGTH
Off-black ground, warm off-white text, muted gold accent. Frame-TV class — no Bootstrap-blue, no traffic-light palette. Two issues to flag: the gold completed-todo square reads at roughly 2:1 against its card background, so at 4 ft the strikethrough alone is carrying "done." And nothing shifts at sunset — 11:25 PM at full brightness punishes a sleepy household.

### 4. Spacing & rhythm
Card radii, internal padding, and gutter rhythm read as a constrained scale — clean. Outer page gutters look like ~25% of viewport in the captured landscape view; that scales to ~270px per side at 1080 portrait, which will feel cramped vertically as a tradeoff. Target 48–72px outer gutters at the production viewport.

### 5. Touch & interaction
Top-fix issue is target size (above). Beyond that: headline rows look tappable but no behavior is visible from the static screenshot — if they're read-only, that's a false affordance, and Norman's rule applies. No pressed/active state inferable; verify it exists once the live build is connectable.

### 6. Information design
Two trust-eroding tells called out in the top fixes (opaque "halfway through" + undated note). One semantic-overlap issue: Today (todos) and Chores are visually identical with overlapping meaning — either merge into one list with a `for: TIM / CAROLINE` column, or differentiate visually so the eye knows what each is for. Headline source labels mix abbreviations (`NYT`, `BK PAPER`, `NPR`) with one full word in caps (`STRATECHERY`); pick a convention.

### 7. Identity & character — STRENGTH
The strongest axis. Names ("Tim and Caroline"), Mabel's age in weeks, byline-on-note, neighborhood weather, neighborhood-specific news source ("BK PAPER"), Park Slope Pediatrics. The dashboard couldn't belong to any other household — exactly the territory Skylight and DAKboard can't reach. This is what justifies the custom build at all; protect it through every reflow.

### 8. Distance readability
Body text in todos, chores, and the calendar list reads ~16–18px in the screenshot — tuned for desktop arm's-length, not wall-at-4ft. Per the rubric, secondary content on this hardware needs ≥24px to be glance-readable. Plan to scale most non-headline text up substantially during the portrait reflow; this is where the largest invisible cost of the landscape design is hiding.

### 9. Ambient hygiene
No day/night palette shift visible — the screen at midnight is still bright white-on-black. No motion or heartbeat that says "this is alive" (the clock ticks; nothing else does); a slow photo crossfade or a pulsing dot on the Mabel tracker would do it without becoming distracting. Burn-in risk on LCD is low, but section labels living in identical pixel positions for 12+ hours are candidates for slight pixel fatigue long-term.

### 10. Wife test
Passes the aesthetic bar — qualified. No admin-panel tells, no Bootstrap remnants, no debug timestamps, no default-emoji icons. Voice is editorial. The two blockers for "passes today" are the photo-card placeholder ("Mabel · week four" with the doubled caption — clearly an in-progress state) and the portrait reflow itself. Both fix in days, not weeks.

## Ideas
1. **Time-of-day-driven views.** A "leaving the house" morning fold (weather + next event + transit alert + diaper-bag check) collapses 30 seconds of phone-checking into one glance. The killer feature an off-the-shelf product can't ship — and the natural payoff for the Claude / OpenClaw integration already in `spec.md`.

2. **Postpartum-arc widget.** The dashboard knows Mabel is 4 weeks; surface week-N normalcy hints (Claude-curated, sourced from a midwife or pediatric blog). Generic family dashboards can't do this; a household-AI-curated one can. Pairs naturally with the "Halfway through" greeting once it has a real temporal referent.

3. **USB lavalier mic + Whisper.cpp on the Pi.** ~$30 hardware, one weekend of wiring. Closes the "I'd add this to a chore list but only my phone can type" loop — turns the wall from a display into a capture surface. Pairs with the Claude / OpenClaw integration the spec already anticipates.

## Verdict
Above v1 bar; type and identity layers are portfolio-grade and worth preserving as the foundation. Doesn't pass the wife test today for two structural reasons: the layout is being designed in landscape but deployed in portrait, and the page assumes scrolling but the wall doesn't scroll. Both are days of work, not weeks. The single biggest strength to protect through the rebuild is the editorial type-and-color system; the single biggest blocker is deciding *what doesn't go on the morning view* so the rest can breathe.
